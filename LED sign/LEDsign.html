Adjustable LED Sign

This is an adjustable LED sign. It's my first go at making something, and so I began with the aim of incorporating woodworking, circuits, and an NC mill. Here is how it was made.








The objective was to create a sign that was adjustable in color. First we start with a schematic for the base. The base is cut off from a spare piece of 2x4. In the middle of one face an impression is going to be milled out.















Here is the block of wood being milled out as well as the end result. Into this cut out will be placed a rectangular piece of acryllic glass. Also there is a cutout (not shown) for storing the knobs and wiring for the LED strip.





















Next comes the acryllic glass. The sign is then created using an NC mill. The mill came with software that lets you enter characters and position them.





















For the lighting, a RGB LED strip is used. Here is the circuit diagram to be followed for the wiring. The plan is to have 3 knobs to control the lighting.








Here is the wiring in progress. Not shown is the switch that is used to connect the LED strip wiring to a receiver for an AC power supply.













The wiring is then stored into the cavity that was carved out with the NC mill. Also, by this point, the block of wood has been sanded down with a belt sander and also by hand. After sanding it down, a wood stain was applied.
















The final product. It plugs into the wall with a standard power supply adapter. First project done!
